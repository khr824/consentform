Research Consent & Release Templates

Guidelines and templates for oral consent, adult releases, minor releases, and parent/guardian permissions.

Oral Consent Guidance

For protocols involving oral consent, following the standard written consent template may not be practical or appropriate for assuring informed consent[cite: 2]. A shorter, more conversational consent script may be more appropriate[cite: 3].

Minimum Required Information

In such cases, the following minimum information must be communicated to the participant[cite: 4]:

  • Introduction explaining who the caller or interviewer is, their affiliation, and organization[cite: 5].
  • A statement that the study involves research[cite: 6].
  • The study purpose[cite: 7].
  • What the participant will be asked to do, the amount of time they will spend, and any planned follow-ups[cite: 8].
  • Details on compensation and any information needed to collect it, such as a mailing or email address[cite: 9].
  • The voluntary nature of participation in the study[cite: 10].
  • Any risks or benefits associated with participating, which can be omitted if there are none[cite: 11].
  • A notice that you are taking notes or recording the data[cite: 12].
  • Clarification on whether the collected information will remain confidential or if identifiers will be kept with the research data[cite: 13].
  • Contact information for the researcher and/or the IRB[cite: 14].
  • An explicit question asking if the participant has any questions you can answer[cite: 15].
  • An explicit request for agreement ("do you agree to participate in this research?") and a record of their response[cite: 16].
Depending on the nature of the study and the participant pool, the researcher may offer other pertinent information to assure that participants are fully informed about the study and any risks or benefits from participating in it[cite: 17].

Performance Agreement and Release (Adults)

This template is used when recording an adult's performance or contribution[cite: 31].

Key Elements Included

  • Acknowledgment that a specific department or division is making media that may include the participant's name, likeness, image, and/or voice[cite: 31].
  • Granting the university the right to make, use, and publish recorded footage across various media formats[cite: 32].
  • Granting the right to edit, mix, duplicate, use, or reuse recordings without restriction regarding changes or alterations[cite: 33].
  • A statement that the university shall have complete ownership of the recordings[cite: 34].
  • Granting the right to distribute, display, broadcast, exhibit, and market the recordings for commercial or non-commercial purposes[cite: 35].
  • Waiver of the right to inspect or approve the finished product or printed matter[cite: 37].
  • Release of the university from any claim or liability, including defamation or copyright infringement[cite: 38].
  • A statement confirming the participant is over the age of eighteen and fully understands the release[cite: 39].

Performance Release (Minors)

This release is entered into by a parent or guardian on behalf of their minor child[cite: 18].

Key Elements Included

  • Acknowledgment that the parent/guardian grants permission for the university to make, use, and publish recorded footage of their son/daughter[cite: 18, 19].
  • Permission to edit, mix, duplicate, and reuse recordings without restriction[cite: 20].
  • A statement that the university retains complete ownership of the program or programs[cite: 21].
  • The right to broadcast, exhibit, market, and distribute the recordings for commercial, non-commercial, or other legal purposes[cite: 22, 23].
  • Waiver of the right for the parent or minor to inspect or approve the finished product[cite: 25].
  • Release of the university from claims or liability, including defamation or copyright infringement[cite: 26].
  • An understanding that the minor will not be named in the credits of the work[cite: 27].
  • A warranty that the signer is over the age of eighteen and has the right to contract for the minor[cite: 28].

Parent/Guardian Permission for Studies Involving Children

In the United States, children are persons who have not attained the legal age for consent, which is any person under 18[cite: 43]. Consent is usually a two-stage process requiring written consent from parents or legal guardians before seeking the child's agreement[cite: 44, 45, 46].

Active vs. Passive Consent

  • Active (Opt-in) Consent: Typically required before conducting research, where parents affirmatively communicate their approval[cite: 50, 51].
  • Passive (Opt-out) Consent: In limited cases, consent is assumed unless the parent communicates otherwise[cite: 54]. This requires that the study poses no risk, the research cannot practicably be carried out otherwise, and the IRB agrees it is appropriate[cite: 55, 56].

Opt-Out Considerations

  • You must justify your use of opt-out consent as necessary and ethically appropriate[cite: 59].
  • For research in schools, include a letter of support for using opt-out consent signed by the principal or a senior administrator[cite: 63].
  • Provide multiple ways for a parent/guardian to inform the researcher that they do not want their child to participate[cite: 67, 68].
  • Give sufficient time for parents to review the information (at least a week) and include a due date for responses[cite: 69].
  • If a substantial number of parents are illiterate or do not read English, offer an appropriate alternative method of communication[cite: 73].

Sample Opt-Out Form Structure

  • Principal Investigator and Faculty Advisor names[cite: 76, 77].
  • Study Title[cite: 78].
  • Description of what the child will do and the time it will take[cite: 82, 84].
  • A statement that there are no known risks and grades will not be affected[cite: 86, 87].
  • Confirmation that the research is anonymous and no identifying information will be collected[cite: 89].
  • Contact information for the researcher and the IRB for questions or concerns[cite: 92].
  • Clear instructions on how to opt out, such as returning a refusal form or sending an email[cite: 95, 96].
  • A "Notification of Refusal" section with a signature line, date, and printed names for the child and parent/guardian[cite: 104, 105, 106, 107, 108, 109].
